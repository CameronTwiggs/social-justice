What is a food desert?

An area where access to affordable nutritious food is limited or even nonexistant. There are no grocery stores within a reasonable distance.

Where does this occur?

All over the United States especially in low income areas in both rural and urban communities. There are 23 million Americans living in food deserts.

What can we do about it?

Through community organizing we can find solutions

Community Gardens

The people's garden

A garden cultivated by a group of people for the benefit of the whole community

How to start a Community Garden

Find a group of like minded people in the community. Asess what resources are available in the area. Decide on a plot of land and pool resources to aquire it. Plan how you will organize it.

What do you plant in a community garden?

The most nutritious fruits and vegetables that are able to grow in moderate climates. For example:

  • Strawberries
  • Kale
  • Radishes
  • Snow Peas
  • Blueberries

Farmer's Markets

A place where local farmers can sell their produce directly to the community

Markets are flexible and relatively easy to set up since they don't require a permanent structure. They improve social ties and create a connection between rural and urban communities. Farmers benefit from selling directly instead of through a middleman. The community benefits from local access to fresh seasonal food.
